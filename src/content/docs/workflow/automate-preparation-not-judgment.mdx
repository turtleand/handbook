---
title: Automate Preparation, Not Judgment
description: Let agents prepare the workshop, but keep final direction, taste, and consequence with humans.
---

AI should reduce the cost of preparation, not remove human responsibility.

The strongest pattern is simple: humans set direction, AI gathers context, structures options, drafts candidates, runs checks, and returns a small decision packet. The human then chooses what becomes real.

This keeps agency in the right place. AI is good at searching, comparing, remembering, formatting, checking, and preparing. Humans are responsible for intent, taste, ethics, risk, public judgment, and irreversible decisions.

Use automation for reflection, triage, summarization, draft branches, test runs, and morning review packets. Do not let it publish, merge, deploy, spend money, email, or change production without explicit approval.

The pattern is especially useful for always-on agents. Overnight work should prepare the table, not eat the meal. A good morning packet is small: top options, what is ready, what needs judgment, what not to do yet, and the recommended first move.

If the AI output creates more pressure, the workflow is wrong. The goal is not more activity. The goal is lower friction for better human decisions.
